HCI Conferences Around the World That We Follow

Conf Name Description Frequency Stars
CHI Covers a wide range of topics related to user experience, design, and interaction with digital systems. Annual A1
UIST Focuses on innovations in user interface technologies, with a mix of hardware, software, and interaction techniques. Annual A1
CSCW Investigates how people work together using digital systems, focusing on collaboration and groupware technologies. Annual A1
IUI Focuses on the integration of AI and HCI, exploring how intelligent systems can support user interfaces and experiences. Annual A1
HCII Includes numerous affiliated conferences addressing specific themes within HCI. Biennial B2
INTERACT Brings together interdisciplinary research from HCI with an emphasis on usability, user experience, and international perspectives. Annual B1
PPIG Focuses on the cognitive aspects of programming and software development, blending insights from psychology, computer science, and human-computer interaction. The conference explores how developers think, learn, and understand code, making it a key event for understanding the human side of programming. Annual B
ICSE Explores how software engineering intersects with HCI, particularly in areas like usability, user-centered design, and the human factors involved in software development processes. Annual A1
VL/HCC Dedicated to human-centric approaches to computing and the use of visual languages for programming and interacting with systems. It covers topics like visual programming languages, end-user programming, and making computing more accessible to non-expert users. Annual B1
FSE FSE is a top-tier conference focused on foundational research in software engineering. Topics span software design, development, and maintenance, with an emphasis on rigorous methods and tools. It also explores human aspects of software engineering, such as developer productivity, usability of engineering tools, and collaboration in software development. Annual A1
EASE Dedicated research on evidence-based software engineering, and its implications for software practice. Annual B4
IEEE CAI CAI is an international conference and exhibition focused on artificial intelligence with specific industry applications. Annual B1
