Blogs

One Useful Thing (Ethan Mollick’s Blog)

A regularly updated blog by educator and researcher Ethan Mollick that provides practical insights into how emerging AI tools impact learning, creativity, and everyday human experiences.

Interacting with Text – Online Resource (Carnegie Mellon University)

A web-based collection of materials examining how people engage with text-based AI systems, focusing on how to design, interpret, and improve human-AI textual interactions through an interdisciplinary lens.

Academic Research to Follow

Stanford Institute for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HAI)

Stanford HAI’s publications, events, and research focus heavily on placing humans at the center of AI development and governance, covering topics like explainability, fairness, and human values in AI-driven decision-making.

MIT Center for Collective Intelligence

This center highlights how groups of people interact with AI-driven systems, enabling new forms of collaboration, governance, and problem-solving.

Harvard Center for Research on Computation and Society (CRCS)

Focused on computational approaches that serve societal needs, CRCS research frequently addresses human-AI interactions, policy implications, and the ethical dimensions of AI use.

Cornell Tech Digital Life Initiative

Interdisciplinary research examining how emerging technologies—including AI—influence human life, with a focus on privacy, ethics, and human-centered design.

Industry Resources to Follow

Microsoft Research – HCI & AI

Explores how to design AI tools that empower people, enhance productivity, and foster inclusive experiences.

Google’s People + AI Research (PAIR)

Shares frameworks, research studies, and design guidelines that center the human experience in AI tools and products.

Partnership on AI

A nonprofit coalition that brings together stakeholders from academia, industry, and civil society to address key AI challenges.

AI Now Institute

Examines the social implications of AI, exploring how people interact with AI in workplaces, justice systems, and broader societal domains.
