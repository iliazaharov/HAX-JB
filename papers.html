Human-AI Experience in Integrated Development Environments:
A Systematic Literature Review

Authors: Agnia Sergeyuk, Ilya Zakharov, Ekaterina Koshchenko, Maliheh Izadi

View the paper on arXiv

Abstract

The integration of AI into Integrated Development Environments (IDEs) is reshaping software development, fundamentally altering how developers interact with their tools. This shift marks the emergence of in-IDE HAX, a field that explores the evolving dynamics of Human-Computer Interaction in AI-assisted coding environments. Despite rapid adoption, research on in-IDE HAX remains fragmented, highlighting the need for a unified overview of current practices, challenges, and opportunities.

We conducted a systematic literature review of 89 studies, summarizing current findings and outlining areas for further investigation. Our synthesis shows that AI-assisted coding boosts productivity but introduces challenges such as verification overhead, automation bias, and over-reliance, particularly among novice developers. Concerns about code correctness, security, and maintainability underline the need for explainability, verification mechanisms, and adaptive user control. While recent advances have propelled the field forward, notable gaps remain— including a lack of longitudinal studies, personalization strategies, and robust AI governance frameworks.

This review lays the foundation for advancing in-IDE HAX research and offers guidance for responsibly integrating AI into software development.
