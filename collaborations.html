AI in Developer Teams

Collaboration with ...
Motivation

GenAI has the potential to transform traditional workflows, communication, and collaboration methods in software development teams. This study explores when, why, and how developers choose AI tools over human interaction, revealing emerging patterns in AI‑mediated team dynamics.

Goals

Investigate how GenAI improves or deteriorates team communication and problem‑solving, and identify usage patterns that correlate with team success or friction. Outputs will inform best practices for integrating AI in collaborative environments.

Longitudinal Analysis of AI Impact

Collaboration with UCI
Motivation

JetBrains hosts vast IDE telemetry that can reveal how developer behaviour shifts after AI integration. Analysing this data will help forecast future trends.

Goals

Derive trends in AI‑affected IDE metrics and map which changes most plausibly stem from AI usage, guiding feature ideation for higher user satisfaction.

User‑Centred In‑IDE HAX

Collaboration with TU Delft
Motivation

Successful tools like Copilot owe much to interface design. IDE users have distinct workflows; injecting LLM‑powered features without disruption poses a rich HCI challenge.

Goals

Embed code generation, explanation, and other LLM practices into the IDE in ways that feel natural and boost productivity rather than distract.
